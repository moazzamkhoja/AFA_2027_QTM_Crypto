## Decision 9: Asset Universe — Point-in-Time Rank Screen, Not a Fixed-Dollar Floor

Human's framing: need a minimum-capital criterion to exclude "penny" tokens without
creating survivorship bias by excluding failed tokens; proposed building the candidate
list via CoinGecko/DeFiLlama/Artemis Analytics, then pulling per-asset transactional and
voting data via Etherscan.

AI proposed refining the minimum-capital criterion: a **fixed nominal-dollar floor is
methodologically unsound** here because total crypto market cap grew by orders of
magnitude from 2015–2026, so any fixed threshold would be miscalibrated at one end of
the sample or the other. Adopted instead a **point-in-time rank/percentile screen**
(default: top 250 by market cap each month, adjustable pending coverage data) with the
rule that **once an asset qualifies, it remains in the panel for the rest of the sample
regardless of subsequent decline or failure** — addressing the penny-token and
survivorship-bias concerns simultaneously. Flagged the common implementation trap of
ranking by *today's* market cap and backfilling (which silently reintroduces
survivorship bias by excluding anything already delisted from current listings) and
required point-in-time historical rankings instead. Also generalized "Etherscan" to
chain-appropriate explorers (Solscan, Snowtrace, Arbiscan, etc.) plus off-chain
governance sources (Snapshot, Tally, Boardroom, DeepDAO) for protocols that vote
off-chain — pure block-explorer reads would miss those votes entirely.

## Decision 10: Sample Period — August 2015 Onward (Not January 2021)

Human pushed back on AI's earlier default suggestion of starting the sample around
January 2021, asking "why just 2021?", and proposed starting from the post-Ethereum
"token economy" era beginning 2015.

**Decision: sample starts August 2015** (the month after Ethereum's mainnet launch,
July 30, 2015), common across the panel, monthly frequency, running to the most
recently completed month. AI's original 2021 suggestion was about balanced-panel
feasibility for governance-token-dependent tests, not a claim that earlier data is
unusable; the spec now requires both the longer 2015+ history and an explicit
year-by-year coverage report so it's visible where governance/voting-dependent tests
(H1b, H2, H3) actually become testable versus where only the coin-only test (H1a) has
sufficient density in the early years. Flagged a concrete worked example of the kind of
landmine this surfaces: Ethereum itself was proof-of-work (no staking mechanism) until
the Beacon Chain deposit contract (~Dec 2020) and the Merge to proof-of-stake (~Sept
2022) — so even ETH, a 2015-era asset, has no staking-based λ channel for most of the
proposed sample window. To be verified against live sources, not assumed.

## Decision 11: Factor Model — Both CAPM and Liu-Tsyvinski-Wu, as Mutual Robustness

Confirmed: build **both** a Liu-Tsyvinski-Wu-style 3-factor crypto model (market, size,
momentum, per their 2022 *Journal of Finance* paper) **and** a CAPM beta with custom
size/momentum controls constructed directly from this paper's own universe, and report
both as robustness checks against each other for the H1/H2/H3 risk-adjusted return
tests, rather than picking one.

## Decision 12: λ-Index Construction — Equal-Weighted Average of Standardized Channels

Confirmed: λ_t is the equal-weighted average of the standardized (z-scored) values of
whichever of the three channels (staking/locking ratio, holding duration, voting
engagement) are observable for a given asset-month. Missing channels are excluded from
the average for that asset-month rather than imputed with zero or a cross-sectional
mean; the count of contributing channels is recorded as a diagnostic.
